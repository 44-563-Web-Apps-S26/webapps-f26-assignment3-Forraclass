Tristen Thompson

Tally Hall

I found Tally Hall when I was in my Junior year of high school through their track "Banana Man" in their first album Marvin's Marvelous Mechanical Museum. "Banana Man" carried two aspects that I would go on to enjoy in Tall Hall's work, namely different sections of each song falling under different genres and image provoking lyrics. I loved listening to many of their tracks on their first album, particular favorites are "The Bidding", "Spring and a Storm", and perhaps their most famous song, "Ruler of Everything." Their next album, Good and Evil carried the same elements that I enjoyed from their first album with a slightly darker and ominous feel. It was after I listened to that album that I found out that Tally Hall had disbanded in 2011, nearly 10 years before I discovered them. Although most members went down different paths away from music after disbanding, Joe Hawley would continue to write music and would go on to create a new album, Hawaii: Part ii featuring the members of the band the following year after their break up. Tally Hall has since wormed its way into the background of many car drives and homework sessions after I first heard "Banana Man" and still makes appearances on occasion in my life.
